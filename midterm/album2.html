About the album


CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST is the seventh studio album by Tyler, The Creator. It is the follow-up to his May 2019 LP, IGOR. The album was first teased by a tag on a trunk that accompanied Tyler at the 2020 Grammy Awards. However, CMIYGL wasn’t assumed to be an album until a billboard was found in Los Angeles in June 2021, which read the album’s title along with a phone number. Over the following days, calling the number played snippets of several tracks on the album, including some that were scrapped. Fans also discovered the album’s website. On June 16, 2021, Tyler released the album’s lead single, “LUMBERJACK,” alongside its video. The next day, he officially confirmed the album’s title and release date via Twitter. The album pays homage to 2000s-era mixtapes that were hosted by DJ Drama, who is also the host of this record. It brings full circle a late 2010 tweet in which Tyler declared that he wanted a Gangsta Grillz mixtape.
