“Implementing a projector substitute in educational institutions for instructors in Antipolo City”

Abstract

This study investigates how educational institutions in Antipolo City are integrating alternate teaching resources, such as projector alternatives. This study explores the viability and efficacy of using projector alternatives to improve teaching and learning environments in response to the growing need for creative teaching approaches. This research intends to offer workable alternatives for the local context by looking at Antipolo City's present technological landscape, educational methods, and the difficulties teachers have when using traditional projectors. It also assesses how projector substitutes affect learning outcomes overall, student engagement, and instructional effectiveness. The study's conclusions are meant to give policymakers, educational technology developers, and stakeholders in education useful information and suggestions to encourage the integration of projector replacement devices in educational environments in Antipolo City.

Key information

Utilizing technology is crucial in today's educational environment to provide engaging and dynamic learning experiences. Replacing outdated projectors with "Smart Boards," which are touch-sensitive panels capable of doing more than essentially showing pictures, is one of the most recent modern innovations in education. It changes standard classrooms into engaging learning environments.
